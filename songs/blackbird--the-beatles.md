## About This Song

Written by Paul McCartney in 1968, reportedly inspired by the civil rights movement in the US. Recorded as a solo acoustic piece, it's one of the most studied fingerpicking songs in rock. The melody and bass line move simultaneously using a single guitar — making it feel complete on its own without any accompaniment.

## Music Theory Notes

The chromatic walkdown (G → F#m → F → E, via chromatic inner voices) is the defining sound of this song — it's a descending bass line under a sustained G melody note, creating mounting tension before resolution. The `A7` chord is a secondary dominant (II7 tonicizing D), which gives the bridge its forward pull. The time signature *feels* like it shifts because McCartney plays rubato — bar lengths stretch and contract to follow the vocal phrase, not a rigid pulse. The piece is solidly in G major (ionian) but the chromatic movement borrows color from outside the key.

## How to Play

Paul plays this with his thumb on bass strings and index/middle on melody — the thumb and fingers alternate independently rather than moving together. The classic opening pattern: thumb hits G on beat 1, finger plucks the melody B string on the "and," repeat. The hardest part is keeping the bass steady while the melody moves chromatically down (G–F#–F–E on the low strings). Learn the bass line and melody separately before combining. Capo is optional — some people use capo 2 to transpose to A major for a slightly brighter tone.
